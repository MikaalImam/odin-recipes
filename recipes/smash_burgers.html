Smash Burger Recipe

Description

Smash burgers are a popular style of hamburger made by pressing a ball of ground beef onto a hot griddle or skillet, creating a thin, crispy-edged patty with a juicy interior. This smashing technique maximizes surface area, enhancing the Maillard reaction for a rich, caramelized crust. Typically served on a soft bun with classic toppings like cheese, pickles, onions, and special sauce, smash burgers are known for their simple yet bold flavor and satisfying texture.

Ingredients

For 4 servings

  • 4 hamburger buns
  • 2 tablespoons of butter
  • 1 pound of ground beef (80% lean - 20% fat)
  • Parchment paper
  • Salt
  • Cheese slices
  • Burger sauce (mixture of ketchap mayo bbq sauce)
  • Onions

Instructions

  1. Prepare the bun sauce to taste before hand.
  2. Thinly slice your onions
  3. Preheat a cast iron skillet or griddle over medium-high heat.
  4. Divide the ground beef into 4 equal portions and roll them into balls.
  5. Place a ball of beef on the hot skillet, cover it with a piece of parchment paper, and use a spatula to smash it down firmly until it's about 1/4 inch thick.
  6. Season the patty with salt and cook for about 2-3 minutes until the edges are crispy and browned.
  7. Flip the patty, add a slice of cheese on top, and cook for another minute until the cheese melts.
  8. Meanwhile, butter the hamburger buns and toast them in another skillet until golden brown.
  9. Add Sauce the sauce to your buns
  10. Assemble the burger by placing the patty on the bottom bun, add your onions, and finish with the top bun.
  11. Serve immediately and enjoy your delicious smash burger!
